Recording Quality & Confidence

How confidence was derived —

Confidence reflects recording signal quality across all three modalities. Low scores may indicate poor lighting, occlusion, or background noise. Scores above 75% are considered reliable.

Formula: Overall confidence = (Face + Arm + Speech) ÷ 3, then mapped to Low (<45%), Moderate (45–75%), or High (≥75%).
Score Derivation Methodology

FAST Risk Index Formula

FAST Risk Index = 0.40 × Face + 0.40 × Arm + 0.20 × Speech
Face (40%)
  • mouth_mean — average horizontal disparity between left/right mouth corner landmarks across frames
  • eye_mean — average vertical height difference between left/right eye aperture
  • Score = weighted combination normalized to [0, 1]; higher = more asymmetric
Arm (40%)
  • drift_metric — mean frame-over-frame downward displacement of the weaker wrist
  • final_height_diff — bilateral elevation gap at end of hold window
  • Score = max(drift_metric, height_diff) scaled to [0, 1]
Speech (20%)
  • pause_var — variance of inter-word silence durations (high = dysarthric hesitation)
  • articulation_dev — standard deviation of per-phoneme energy envelope
  • mfcc_var — mean variance across 13 MFCCs (spectral instability)
  • Score = weighted sum of all three, bounded [0, 1]
Quality Confidence — each channel independently estimates recording quality via MediaPipe landmark visibility scores (face/arm) and SNR/spectral flatness (speech). Overall confidence = arithmetic mean; mapped to Low (<45%), Moderate (45–75%), High (≥75%).
Risk thresholds: Low < 0.3  |  Moderate 0.3–0.6  |  High ≥ 0.6
